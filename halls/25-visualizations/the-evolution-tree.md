                                   KEY INSIGHTS


    ╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
    ║                                                                                   ║
    ║  EVOLUTIONARY PRINCIPLES:                                                         ║
    ║                                                                                   ║
    ║  • Descent with Modification: Each generation slightly different                 ║
    ║  • Natural Selection: Survival of the fit enough                                 ║
    ║  • Genetic Drift: Random changes matter                                          ║
    ║  • Mutation: Copying errors create variation                                     ║
    ║  • Speciation: Populations diverge until reproductively isolated                 ║
    ║  • Extinction: Most branches end (but DNA information persists in survivors)     ║
    ║  • Convergent Evolution: Similar solutions arise independently                   ║
    ║  • Co-evolution: Species shape each other                                        ║
    ║                                                                                   ║
    ║  MAJOR TRANSITIONS:                                                               ║
    ║                                                                                   ║
    ║  1. Replicating molecules (RNA World)              ~4.0 BYA                      ║
    ║  2. Cellular life (LUCA)                           ~3.8 BYA                      ║
    ║  3. Prokaryotes (Bacteria & Archaea)               ~3.8 BYA                      ║
    ║  4. Photosynthesis (Cyanobacteria)                 ~3.5 BYA                      ║
    ║  5. Eukaryotes (complex cells)                     ~2.1 BYA                      ║
    ║  6. Sexual reproduction                            ~1.2 BYA                      ║
    ║  7. Multicellular organisms                        ~1.0 BYA                      ║
    ║  8. Animals (Metazoans)                            ~800 MYA                      ║
    ║  9. Cambrian Explosion (body plans diversify)      ~541 MYA                      ║
    ║ 10. Vertebrates                                    ~525 MYA                      ║
    ║ 11. Land plants                                    ~470 MYA                      ║
    ║ 12. Land animals (tetrapods)                       ~375 MYA                      ║
    ║ 13. Amniotes (eggs on land)                        ~312 MYA                      ║
    ║ 14. Dinosaurs                                      ~230 MYA                      ║
    ║ 15. Mammals                                        ~225 MYA                      ║
    ║ 16. Birds (from dinosaurs)                         ~150 MYA                      ║
    ║ 17. Flowering plants (angiosperms)                 ~140 MYA                      ║
    ║ 18. Primates                                       ~85 MYA                       ║
    ║ 19. Homo sapiens (us!)                             ~300 KYA                      ║
    ║                                                                                   ║
    ║  MASS EXTINCTIONS (Pruning Events):                                               ║
    ║                                                                                   ║
    ║  • Ordovician-Silurian: 444 MYA (86% marine species lost)                        ║
    ║  • Late Devonian: 375 MYA (75% species lost)                                     ║
    ║  • Permian-Triassic: 252 MYA (96% marine, 70% terrestrial - "The Great Dying")  ║
    ║  • Triassic-Jurassic: 201 MYA (80% species lost)                                ║
    ║  • Cretaceous-Paleogene: 66 MYA (76% species including non-avian dinosaurs)     ║
    ║  • Holocene (ongoing): Anthropocene extinction - humans causing 6th event       ║
    ║                                                                                   ║
    ║  CURRENT STATISTICS:                                                              ║
    ║                                                                                   ║
    ║  • ~8.7 million species alive today (estimated)                                  ║
    ║  • ~1.2 million species formally described                                       ║
    ║  • Insects: ~1 million species (most diverse animals)                            ║
    ║  • ~99.9% of all species that ever existed are now extinct                       ║
    ║  • Humans: 1 species among millions, arrived in last 0.008% of life's history   ║
